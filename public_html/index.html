Work

ESTsoft Inc.

Localization Manager • May 2011 - 2016

When I started working at ESTsoft, there were only five employees at the time. In a small software publishing environment, you have to be pretty dynamic to successfully fill the many required rolls off a functional office. I wore all sorts of shoes during the first couple of years. This included customer service, pc tech, software testing, web design, event planning, marketing, business to business, you name it. All throughout my career, I had urged that the company service the game in South America to target an untapped audience via localization practices. When I finally got the green light, I was tasked localizing CABAL 1 to Spanish. The game quickly became a hit in South America, and ESTsoft rapidly began to hire on translation staff to publish the game in multiple languages, and countries. I spent my last three years at ESTsoft as the localization manager, ensuring the localization of multiple products, training localization staff and also Twitch streaming on the side. My biggest project was the complete localization of CABAL 2 from Korean to English, a massive project that took the better part of a year. Since then, I've moved on to start up freelance web design, and study software development.
